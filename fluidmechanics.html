Derive and explain Bernoulli's equation with its assumptions and applications

Bernoulli's Equation: A fundamental equation in fluid mechanics describing the conservation of energy in a flowing fluid.

P/œÅg + V¬≤/2g + z = constant

Derivation: From Euler's equation of motion along a streamline:

dp/œÅ + V dV + g dz = 0
Integrating: ‚à´dp/œÅ + V¬≤/2 + gz = constant
For incompressible fluid: P/œÅ + V¬≤/2 + gz = constant
Dividing by g: P/œÅg + V¬≤/2g + z = constant

Assumptions:

  • Steady flow: No change with time
  • Incompressible fluid: Constant density
  • Inviscid flow: Negligible viscosity
  • Along a streamline: Valid only along same streamline
  • No shaft work: No pumps or turbines
  • No heat transfer: Isothermal conditions

Applications:

  • Venturimeter: Flow rate measurement
  • Pitot tube: Velocity measurement
  • Airfoil design: Lift generation
  • Carburetors: Fuel mixing in engines
  • Atomizers: Spray formation
  • Siphons: Liquid transfer

Limitations:

  • Not applicable for viscous flows
  • Cannot predict flow separation
  • Invalid across streamlines
  • Not valid for compressible flow at high Mach numbers

Explain the Moody chart and its use in determining friction factor for pipe flow

Moody Chart: A graphical representation showing the Darcy friction factor (f) as a function of Reynolds number (Re) and relative roughness (Œµ/D).

Darcy-Weisbach Equation: hf = f (L/D) (V¬≤/2g)
where: f = friction factor, L = pipe length, D = diameter, V = velocity

Chart Characteristics:

  • X-axis: Reynolds number (Re) on logarithmic scale
  • Y-axis: Friction factor (f) on logarithmic scale
  • Curves: Each curve represents different relative roughness (Œµ/D)
  • Regions: Laminar, transitional, and turbulent zones

Key Regions:

1. Laminar Region (Re < 2000):

f = 64/Re (straight line on log-log plot)
  • Independent of pipe roughness
  • All fluids follow same line
  • Theoretical result from Hagen-Poiseuille equation

2. Critical Region (2000 < Re < 4000):

  • Unpredictable flow behavior
  • Transition between laminar and turbulent
  • Usually avoided in design

3. Turbulent Region (Re > 4000):

  • Smooth pipes: Follow Blasius equation: f = 0.316/Re0.25 (Re < 105)
  • Rough pipes: f depends on relative roughness Œµ/D
  • Complete turbulence: f becomes constant (horizontal lines)

  6. Use f in Darcy-Weisbach equation to find head loss
