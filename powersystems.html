Explain the concept of symmetrical components and its application in fault analysis

▼

Symmetrical Components: A mathematical method developed by C.L. Fortescue to simplify the analysis of unbalanced three-phase systems by transforming them into three balanced systems.

Transformation:

Positive Sequence (V₁): Balanced system with normal phase sequence (abc)

Negative Sequence (V₂): Balanced system with reverse phase sequence (acb)

Zero Sequence (V₀): Three phasors equal in magnitude and phase

Where: a = 1∠120° (complex operator)

Applications in Fault Analysis:

  • Single Line-to-Ground Fault: All three sequence networks connected in series
  • Line-to-Line Fault: Positive and negative sequence networks connected in parallel
  • Double Line-to-Ground Fault: All three sequence networks connected in parallel
  • Three-Phase Fault: Only positive sequence network (balanced fault)

Describe the working principle of different types of circuit breakers

▼

Circuit Breakers: Devices designed to interrupt fault currents and protect electrical equipment.

1. Oil Circuit Breakers:

  • Working: Contacts separate under oil, arc vaporizes oil creating hydrogen gas bubble which cools and deionizes arc path
  • Types: Bulk Oil (dead tank) & Minimum Oil (live tank)
  • Applications: Up to 245 kV, indoor/outdoor substations

2. Vacuum Circuit Breakers:

  • Working: Contacts separate in high vacuum (10⁻⁶ to 10⁻¹⁰ torr), arc extinguished at first current zero due to rapid dielectric recovery
  • Advantages: No maintenance, compact, fire-safe, long life
  • Applications: Medium voltage (11-33 kV), indoor switchgear

3. SF6 Circuit Breakers:

  • Working: SF6 gas flows across arc, captures electrons forming negative ions, excellent dielectric and arc-quenching properties
  • Types: Puffer type (self-blast) & Gas blast
  • Applications: EHV systems (72.5 kV to 800 kV)

4. Air Blast Circuit Breakers:

  • Working: High pressure air blast (20-30 bar) directed across arc, removes heat and ions
  • Applications: Older EHV installations, being replaced by SF6
